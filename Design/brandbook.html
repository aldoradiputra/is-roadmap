01 · Foundation

Six principles

Indonesia System is software that 50,000 people will use for eight hours a day. Every decision in this book serves that fact.

01

Calm density

Show a lot of information without feeling overwhelming. Tables and forms are first-class citizens, not last-resort.

02

Honest, unornamented

No gradients-as-decoration, no glassmorphism, no AI-shimmer. If a pixel doesn't earn its place, it leaves.

03

Confident typography

One family does the work. Hierarchy comes from weight and size, not from a second display face.

04

Indonesian-rooted, internationally credible

At home in Jakarta, believable in any boardroom. The Indonesian-ness comes from craft, not ornament.

05

Built for long sessions

Eyes don't tire. Contrast is calibrated; whitespace is generous; movement is rare.

06

Live, not static

Subtle indicators of real-time activity — presence, updates, syncs — baked into the design without taking over.

02 · Logo system

A mark that holds the equator

Three directions were explored. We committed to the architectural mark: a square monogram of stacked bars, crossed by a single indigo hairline — a quiet reference to the equator, which Indonesia straddles. The mark is built from the same primitives as a spreadsheet row. That is the point.

A · the chosen direction · architectural bars + equator
B · explored · bold typographic monogram (rejected: too many products use this)
C · explored · seal/coin (rejected: reads governmental, not corporate)

Clear space & minimum size

Clear space around the wordmark equals the height of the "I" stem on all sides — measured against the mark itself, not against the type. Minimum sizes: 16px for the monogram (favicon), 120px for the wordmark (so "Indonesia System" stays readable). On dark surfaces, use the on-dark variant — the equator hairline keeps its indigo color.

03 · Color

Navy anchors. Indigo acts. Teal & amber whisper.

A locked palette of seven colors plus neutrals. Treat the palette like a single sentence: navy is the subject, indigo is the verb, teal and amber are punctuation.

Usage rules

  • Never combine more than two phase colors in a single screen region.
  • Indigo is the dominant interactive color. Teal and amber appear only as semantic status — success, warning. Never as decoration.
  • Background is off-white #F8F9FB. Surfaces are pure white. There are no tinted card backgrounds.
  • Hover states are computed darker, not from a separate token. Press states darken further and translate 1px down.

04 · Typography

One family. Everything.

08 · Motion

Short, confident, rare.

Animation in IS is a system-status signal, not entertainment. Four durations, two curves, a short list of what animates and an even shorter list of what does not.

What animates

  • Panel and modal entrance/exit (220ms · ease)
  • Hover scale on cards · max 1.02
  • Real-time data pulses · single 600ms opacity dip
  • Loading skeletons · 2.4s opacity pulse, no shimmer

What does not

  • Text content. Numbers don't count up; words don't type.
  • Table-row reorders during data refresh.
  • Anything for longer than 350ms.

10 · Don'ts

A short list of bright lines

Most rules in this book are described as positives. These are the negatives.

Don't recolor the logo.The mark is navy with an indigo equator. Single-color black or white only — never green, never red, never tinted.
Don't add a second display font.Inter does the entire job. Adding a serif headline face breaks the architectural feel.
Don't decorate with gradients.No background fades, no shimmering buttons, no glass. Flat colors only.
Don't add accent borders to cards.The colored-left-border treatment is a SaaS template cliché. Status lives in badges, not borders.
Don't use Indonesian visual clichés.No batik patterns, no Garuda, no red-and-white flag motifs, no Pancasila references. Indonesian-ness comes from the work.
Don't animate text.Numbers never count up; words never type out. Skeletons are for loading; animation is for state transitions.
